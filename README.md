Intelligent Handover Management for Hybrid TN–NTN Mobility in 6G Architectures, VTC 2026 


Abstract: Seamless mobility across terrestrial and non-terrestrial networks (TN-NTN) is a cornerstone of 6G connectivity, yet managing handovers (HOs) in such hybrid architectures remains an open challenge. Rapid LEO satellite motion, heterogeneous propagation, and overlapping multi-layer coverage render classical threshold-based HO mechanisms inadequate, often causing excessive switching, ping-pong oscillations, and service outages. This paper proposes a unified reinforcement learning (RL) framework that formulates HO control as a single Markov decision process spanning both TN and NTN cells. We comparatively evaluate three RL algorithms—Proximal Policy Optimization (PPO), Advantage Actor-Critic (A2C), and Deep Q-Network (DQN)—together with Best-RSRP and Random baselines on two realistic trace-driven TN-NTN propagation scenarios derived from real base station deployments in Luxembourg. All agents are assessed on held-out test segments unseen during training. In the gNB-dominated scenario (21~cells), PPO performs only 2 targeted HOs with zero ping-pong and zero failure, outperforming DQN (40 HOs, 35% ping-pong) and A2C (which defaults to the LEO satellite, leaving a 29dB quality gap). In the coverage-critical NTN scenario (10~cells), all RL agents correctly identify LEO as the primary serving cell, while DQN additionally tracks transient gNB peaks. These results demonstrate that policy-gradient methods---particularly PPO---deliver topology-aware, stable HO policies well suited to 6G TN-NTN deployments.
